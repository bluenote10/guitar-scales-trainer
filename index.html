Guitar Scales Trainer – Exercises

    This is a small app that helps to master the guitar fretboard by visualizing various scales and patterns. The exercises force you to visualize different aspects of a scale, like horizontally or vertically related scales, musically related scales, and so on. The idea is to challenge the brain in all sorts of different ways to gain a deeper understanding of the guitar fretboard. The exercises are randomly generated to make to practice all aspects equally, and avoiding to become for instance only familiar with moving a scale upwards, but not downwards. All exercises can be done without a guitar, but of course if can even be more effective to practice the scale along on the instrument to add muscle memory to the process.

  • Diatonic – Neighboring scales

    This exercises helps to improve the relationship between neighboring diatonic scales in the next upper/lower position.

    Scales: Diatonic (three notes per string)

  • Diatonic – From two strings to full scale pattern

    This exercises helps to improve fretboard orientation based on small, two-string fragments.

    Scales: Diatonic (three notes per string)

  • Diatonic – Circle of fifths neighboring scales

    This exercises helps to improve the relationship between diatonic scales in the same position, which are neighbors in the circle of fifths.

    Scales: Diatonic (three notes per string)

  • Diatonic – Two strings up and down the neck

    This exercises helps to improve moving a scale up and down the neck on just two strings.

    Scales: Diatonic (three notes per string)

  • Diatonic – Intervals

    This exercises helps to improve thinking in intervals.

    Scales: Diatonic (three notes per string)
